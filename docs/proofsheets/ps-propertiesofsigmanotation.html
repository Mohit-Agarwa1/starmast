Before reading this proof sheet, it is recommended that you read Guide: Introduction to sigma notation and [Guide: Proof by induction].

Proof of properties of sigma notation

Distributivity

Distributivity

Proof of distributivity

You can see this is true by writing the entire sum out, like this:

\[ \begin{array}{rcl} \sum_{i=k}^nCa_i &=& Ca_k + Ca_{k+1} + Ca_{k+2} + \ldots + Ca_n \\ &=& C(a_k + a_{k+1} + a_{k+2} + \ldots + a_n) \\ &=& C\sum_{i=k}^n a_k \end{array} \]

Proof of combining and decomposing sums

Similar to the distributive property, you can show this is true by writing the entire sum out:

\[ \begin{array}{rcl} \sum_{i=k}^na_i + \sum_{i=k}^n b_i &=& (a_{k} + a_{k+1} + \ldots + a_n) + (b_k + b_{k+1} + \ldots + b_n) \\ & = & (a_k + b_k) + (a_{k+1} + b_{k+1}) + \ldots + (a_n + b_n) \\[0.5em] & = & \sum_{i=k}^n (a_k + b_k). \end{array} \]

In a similar way, you can show that \(\sum_{i=k}^n a_i - \sum_{i=k}^n b_i = \sum_{i=k}^n(a_i-b_i)\) is also true.
